**Social Media Do's and Don'ts for College Students**



&#x20;**DO's**



* Keep your profiles professional. Use a clear, appropriate profile photo and write a bio that reflects your academic and professional interests.  
* Use privacy settings. Regularly review who can see your posts, photos, and personal information on all platforms. 
* Give credit when sharing. If you share someone else's article, image, or idea, always tag the original creator or cite the source. 
* Engage positively and constructively. If you disagree with someone online, express your view respectfully with facts and reasoning. 
* Report harmful content. If you see bullying, hate speech, or misinformation, use the platform's reporting tools rather than engaging with it. 



&#x20;**DON'TS**



* Don't share personal or financial information publicly. Never post your phone number, address, Aadhaar number, or bank details on any social platform. 
* Don't engage with or forward unverified news. Check facts on credible sources (PIB Fact Check, Reuters, etc.) before sharing news that seems surprising or alarming. 
* Don't post in anger or frustration. Emotional posts made in the heat of the moment can cause lasting reputational damage — wait 24 hours before posting about a conflict. 
* Don't overshare your real-time location. Avoid posting live locations, tagging yourself at your hostel or home, or sharing travel plans publicly. 
* Don't respond to strangers asking for personal help or money. Scammers often pose as peers, professors, or well-wishers on social platforms.
