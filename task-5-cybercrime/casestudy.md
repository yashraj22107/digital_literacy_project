# Task 5 – Cybercrime Case Study: UPI/Online Payment Fraud

## What is UPI/Online Payment Fraud?

UPI (Unified Payments Interface) fraud is a type of financial cybercrime where criminals trick victims into authorising money transfers from their bank accounts through deception, impersonation, or technical manipulation. It is one of the fastest-growing cybercrimes in India, affecting students, homemakers, and working professionals alike.

## How It Typically Happens (Step-by-Step)

1. **Initial contact:** The fraudster contacts the victim via phone call, WhatsApp, or social media, posing as a bank employee, customer support agent, or seller on an online marketplace.
2. **Building trust:** They claim there is an urgent problem — a blocked account, a cashback offer, or a technical issue — that requires immediate action.
3. **Requesting a UPI ID or QR code scan:** The fraudster sends a payment request or a QR code and tells the victim to "scan to receive money." In reality, scanning the QR code sends money *out*, not in.
4. **OTP extraction:** In some cases, they convince the victim to share the OTP (One-Time Password) sent by the bank, claiming it is needed for "verification."
5. **Money is transferred:** Once the victim scans the code or shares the OTP, the fraudster immediately withdraws funds, often from multiple accounts in quick succession.
6. **Disappearance:** The phone number is switched off and the UPI account is abandoned or already linked to a fake identity.

## Who is Usually Targeted?

Students buying second-hand items on OLX or Facebook Marketplace, people selling items online, users responding to fake job offers, and elderly individuals unfamiliar with UPI mechanics are common targets.

## Consequences

Victims lose money instantly, often with little chance of recovery. Emotional consequences include stress and embarrassment. In severe cases, victims have lost their entire savings or taken on debt.

**Source:** National Cyber Crime Reporting Portal (cybercrime.gov.in), Times of India cybercrime reports (2024).
