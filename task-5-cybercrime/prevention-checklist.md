# Stay Safe Online – Prevention Checklist for College Students

*Prepared for CSE0001 – Digital Literacy | VIT Bhopal University*

---

## General Online Safety

- [ ] **Use strong, unique passwords** for every account. Use a mix of uppercase letters, numbers, and symbols. Never reuse the same password across multiple platforms.
- [ ] **Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA)** on all important accounts — email, banking apps, social media, and university portals.
- [ ] **Keep your phone and apps updated.** Security patches are released regularly and protect against newly discovered vulnerabilities.
- [ ] **Do not click on unknown links** received via SMS, WhatsApp, or email — even if they appear to come from a known contact. Verify through a separate call before clicking.
- [ ] **Use a secure, private Wi-Fi network** for banking and sensitive tasks. Avoid using public Wi-Fi (cafes, bus stands, malls) for any financial transaction.
- [ ] **Lock your phone with a PIN or biometric** and enable remote wipe in case it is lost or stolen.

## Financial / UPI Safety (Mandatory Tips)

- [ ] **Remember: QR codes and payment requests SEND money, they do not receive it.** Never scan a QR code from a stranger who claims to be sending you money.
- [ ] **Never share your OTP, UPI PIN, or CVV with anyone** — including people claiming to be bank employees. Banks never ask for OTPs over the phone.

## Social and Identity Safety

- [ ] **Do not share personal details** (Aadhaar number, phone number, home address) on social media or public forums.
- [ ] **Verify the identity of any online seller or buyer** before transferring money. Use cash-on-delivery options when possible for marketplace transactions.

## How to Report Cybercrime in India

| Channel | Details |
|---------|---------|
| **National Cyber Crime Portal** | [cybercrime.gov.in](https://cybercrime.gov.in) — file an online complaint 24x7 |
| **Cyber Crime Helpline** | **1930** — call immediately if you have been financially defrauded |
| **Local Police Station** | File an FIR for serious cases of identity theft or fraud |

> **Important:** If you have lost money through UPI fraud, call **1930 within the first hour** — early reporting increases the chance of fund recovery.
